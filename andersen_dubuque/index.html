How this works

The steps below are the phases of your project. As each phase opens, click it to learn what is happening, why, and what it means for you. Some steps are locked until the project reaches them — that is normal. Your input matters most during the survey, so watch for that phase.

This portal backs up — it does not replace — the bulletins, meetings, and emails you will get in person. If anything here is unclear, the Human Resources Department is your point of contact.

Andersen brought in Shiftwork Solutions, an independent consulting firm that has worked with hundreds of 24/7 operations over more than three decades, to take a structured look at the shift schedules here in Dubuque.

The goal is not to hand down a schedule from the outside. It is to understand how the current schedule affects both the operation and your life away from work — and then to build options around what the workforce actually values. That is why a confidential survey is a central part of this process: your preferences go on the table, not assumptions about them.

Shiftwork Solutions stays neutral. The firm works for a good outcome for the operation and the people in it, and everything moves in the open — through meetings, written materials, and this portal.

Here is the shape of the engagement. Exact dates will be shared in your meetings and bulletins.

  • Kickoff & data collection — the team meets with leadership and supervisors, learns how each area runs today, and gathers the operating information the analysis needs.
  • Analysis & strategy — the team works through the data off-site, then reviews schedule strategy with management.
  • Employee survey — you meet the team, learn about the process, and complete the Shift Schedule Survey.
  • Survey results — results are tabulated overall and by group, and the best schedule options to pursue are decided.
  • Final options — schedule options and any pay and work policies are documented.
  • Selection — options are presented, questions are answered, and the workforce indicates its preference.
  • Follow-up — a follow-up survey checks how the new schedule is working.

At the heart of this project is the Shift Schedule Survey. In a series of short meetings, you will get familiar with the process and then have your say on the things that shape day-to-day life on a schedule — topics like overtime, start times, shift lengths, and schedule patterns.

There are no right or wrong answers. The survey is simply how your preferences get counted, so the options that come out of this reflect the workforce rather than guesses about it. After each meeting, the consultant is available for individual questions.

Spouses and family are welcome to understand the process too — you can point them to this same page.

Once everyone has weighed in, the results are tabulated — overall and by group, such as department and shift. When this phase opens, you will be able to see a short highlights summary here: where the workforce is aligned, and where there are trade-offs the team will work through.

This is a one-page overview of the key takeaways. The full survey detail stays with the project team, but the highlights give you a clear sense of what the workforce said.

With the survey results in hand, the team develops the schedule options that best fit what the workforce values and what the operation needs. When this phase opens, you will see how the options are presented and how the selection works.

You will get to see the options, have your questions answered in person, and indicate your preference. Nothing is decided without the workforce having its say.

When the final schedule options are ready, this is where you can dig into the details of the implementation package — how the schedule works, and how policies like overtime and holiday pay are calculated.
