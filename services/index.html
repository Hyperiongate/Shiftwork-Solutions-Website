In Simplest Terms

We Do Three Things

Every engagement — regardless of size, industry, or complexity — follows the same proven structure. We analyze your current operation to understand what's really happening. We engage your workforce so the people affected by the schedule have a genuine voice in shaping it. And we implement — designing the solution, building the policies, and supporting the transition until it holds on its own.

1

Analyze the Current Situation

We collect operating data, walk your facility, meet with leadership and supervisors, and build a clear picture of how the current schedule is performing — what it's costing, where it's creating strain, and what opportunities exist. The business analysis, cost modeling, and benchmarking against our proprietary database give you facts, not assumptions.

2

Engage the Workforce

We survey every affected employee to understand what they actually value — shift preferences, overtime appetite, work-life priorities, and satisfaction with the current system. This isn't a formality. The survey data shapes the schedule options we develop, and the involvement builds the buy-in that makes implementation succeed. People support what they helped create.

3

Implement

We design schedule options that meet your operational requirements and reflect what the workforce told us. We develop the pay and time-off policies that align with the new pattern. We present the options to employees, facilitate the selection process, and support the transition. The workforce chooses — and the schedule they pick becomes their schedule.

Core Services

Six Ways We Help 24/7 Operations

Every engagement is customized. These are the building blocks — most projects combine several into an integrated solution. We scope work before we price it, so you only pay for what your operation actually needs.

The six services below address every dimension of the schedule problem: technical design, financial modeling, employee engagement, change implementation, and the policy framework that holds it together once we leave.

Schedule Design & Analysis

Custom shift patterns that balance operational coverage, labor costs, fatigue management, and employee preferences. We don't pick from a menu — we design schedules around your specific coverage needs, demand patterns, and workforce reality.

8-hour, 12-hour, fixed, rotating, hybrid, semi-continuous, fly-in/fly-out, and more

Staffing Strategy

Right-size your workforce for the schedule. We model staffing scenarios for hiring, attrition, and growth — so you know exactly how many people you need before committing to a pattern. No guesswork.

Headcount modeling, relief crew sizing, skill-balance analysis, vacancy planning

Change Management

This is the 80% that determines success or failure. Employee-centered implementation that builds buy-in before the first shift rotates. Communication plans, on-shift meetings, leadership coaching, and the employee choice process that makes the schedule theirs.

Communication planning, on-shift meetings, employee orientation, schedule selection facilitation

Employee Engagement Surveys

Our proprietary employee engagement tool is purpose-built for shiftwork operations — not adapted from a generic office survey. It captures what shift workers actually think about their schedules, overtime, work-life balance, supervision, and communication. Every engagement is customized to your operation's specific circumstances, and every result is benchmarked against our normative database from hundreds of facilities across 16+ industries.

Customizable survey instrument, normative benchmarking, demographic breakouts, trend tracking

Cost Analysis

True fully loaded cost of every schedule option — not just wages, but benefits, paid time off, training, and administrative overhead. Overtime projections, benefit impacts, and ROI modeling. Numbers your CFO will trust because they account for what most analyses miss.

Fully loaded labor costs, adverse cost analysis, scenario comparison, break-even modeling

Policy Development

Schedule changes cascade into vacation accrual, holiday pay, overtime distribution, shift differentials, and call-off procedures. We develop work and pay policies that align with the new schedule and prevent the conflicts that erode trust after implementation.

Vacation and holiday policies, shift differential structures, overtime distribution, call-off procedures

Challenges We Solve

What Brings Operations Leaders to Our Door

If any of these sound familiar, you are dealing with a schedule-driven problem — and it is solvable.

Rising unit costs

Chronic overtime, excess headcount, and misaligned labor supply are driving up the cost of every unit you produce.

High turnover on off-shifts

You can't keep people on nights and weekends. Competitors with better schedules are poaching your trained workforce.

Expanding to 24/7

The leap from 5-day to continuous operations is the biggest workforce change most facilities face. Employees fear the unknown.

Coverage gaps and absenteeism

Unplanned absences, FMLA, and skill imbalances leave machines idle and supervisors scrambling every week.

Fatigue and safety concerns

Long shifts, early starts, and poor rotation patterns are degrading alertness and increasing incident risk.

A past change went badly

You tried to change schedules before and it failed — or succeeded technically but destroyed trust. You need a different approach.

Too many schedules

Departments running different patterns create coordination problems, skill imbalances, and management headaches.

Seasonal or volatile demand

Fixed schedules can't flex with your workload. You need a system that absorbs variability without burning people out.

Greenfield or expansion

New facility, new line, or consolidation. You have one chance to set the schedule right from the start.

How It Works

A Typical Engagement, Week by Week

Every project is different, but the structure is proven. We do the heavy lifting. You keep running your operation.

P
Pre-Project

Background Data Collection

Before anyone sets foot on site, we collect historical operating data, current schedule descriptions for every department, planned work levels, and cost information. We arrive prepared.

1
Week 1 · On-Site

Discovery & Kickoff

Project kickoff with leadership and supervisors. Meetings with each work area to understand their role, current schedule, requirements, and issues. Individual sessions with the controller, HR, and safety. This week is about listening.

2
Week 2 · Off-Site

Business Analysis

Analyze everything collected in Week 1. Build the business case. Develop cost, benefit, and risk analysis. Prepare preliminary findings and schedule modeling.

3
Week 3 · On-Site

Analysis Review & Survey Prep

Review business analysis with leadership. Finalize the workforce survey instrument based on what we learned about your specific operation.

4
Week 4 · On-Site

Employee Orientation & Survey

Employee orientation and survey meetings. Every affected employee participates. Consultants available for individual questions. This is where workforce involvement begins in earnest.

5
Week 5 · Off-Site

Survey Processing & Report

Process survey results. Tabulate by overall results and by demographic groups — departments, shifts, family care responsibilities. Build the comparison report with normative benchmarks.

6
Week 6 · On-Site

Options Development

Present survey results to management. Develop schedule options and pay policies based on what the survey revealed. Begin implementation documentation.

7
Week 7 · On-Site

Employee Choice & Implementation

Present options to all affected personnel. Distribute implementation documentation. Collect schedule preference forms. The workforce chooses — and the schedule they pick becomes their schedule.

✓
Follow-Up

Post-Implementation Review

Conduct a follow-up survey after implementation. Measure satisfaction, identify any issues, and make adjustments. We don't disappear after Week 7.
