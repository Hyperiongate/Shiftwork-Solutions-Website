Our Philosophy

Win-Win-Win Is Not a Slogan

We start from the premise that the best schedule for your operation and the best schedule for your employees are usually the same schedule. When these interests align — and with the right design they almost always can — you get lower turnover, less mandatory overtime, higher productivity, and a workforce that becomes a competitive advantage rather than a constant source of friction.

This is not idealism. It reflects how schedule design actually works in practice. A schedule that generates chronic mandatory overtime will drive away the employees you most want to keep. A schedule that provides insufficient coverage will create operational chaos that no amount of supervisory effort can fix. The math and the human element are inseparable, and treating them as separate problems is why so many schedule changes fail.

We also believe that the people doing the work have insight that no outside consultant can replicate. Employee involvement is not a concession to labor relations — it is how you get better information, build genuine buy-in, and ensure the solution accounts for realities that only show up on the floor at 2 AM. Every engagement we lead is designed around this principle.

"The more the workforce understands and participates, the better the outcome. A collaborative process with well-informed participants leads to results that stretch beyond the boundaries of any single project."

How We Work

From Complexity to Clarity

Every engagement follows a structured three-phase process. The phases build on each other — what we learn in the assessment shapes what we design, and what we design determines how we deliver. Skipping steps is how engagements go wrong, so we don't skip them.

Phase 1
Assess
  • Cost-benefit and ROI modeling across multiple scenarios
  • Workforce surveys to identify preferences and pain points
  • Benchmarking against our proprietary scheduling database
  • Analysis of current schedule's operational and workforce impact
Phase 2
Design
  • Schedule options built to support both operations and people
  • Iterative modeling with leadership input
  • Work and pay policy alignment for each option
  • Communication plan for transparency and alignment
Phase 3
Deliver
  • Rollout support from senior consultants
  • Employee education and implementation package
  • Work and pay policy development and documentation
  • Ongoing guidance to ensure long-term results

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of operations do you work with?
Any operation running shifts — manufacturing, distribution, food processing, mining, chemicals, refining, utilities, automotive, pharmaceuticals, and more. We work with organizations ranging from single-site operations to multi-facility enterprises, in both union and non-union environments.
How long does a typical engagement take?
Most engagements run 3 to 6 months from initial assessment through implementation. The timeline depends on the complexity of the operation, the number of employees affected, union negotiation requirements, and how quickly decisions can be made internally. We'll give you a realistic timeline estimate after an initial conversation.
Do you work with unions?
Yes. We have extensive experience in union environments and understand both the contractual constraints and the political dynamics involved. Our approach — employee surveys, transparent communication, genuine workforce involvement — tends to build the kind of support that makes union ratification achievable.
What does the process cost?
We operate on a fixed-fee model, so there are no hourly billing surprises. Fees vary based on the scope of the engagement — number of employees, number of sites, complexity of the current schedule, and implementation requirements. We provide a specific proposal after an initial consultation, which is free.
Will we need new software or technology?
No. Our process requires no new scheduling software, no technology integration, and no changes to your existing systems. We work with the tools you already have.
How is Shiftwork Solutions different from a large consulting firm?
Depth of specialization and who does the work. Large firms staff projects with junior analysts supervised by a distant partner. At Shiftwork Solutions, a senior partner leads every engagement from start to finish. That partner has done this hundreds of times — which means they know what to look for, what questions to ask, and what solutions actually hold up after implementation.
